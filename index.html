Explore Our Destinations

AULI

Auli, in Uttarakhand’s Garhwal Himalayas, offers breathtaking views of snow-clad peaks, lush meadows, and thrilling skiing slopes. Known as a winter wonderland, its serene beauty, Auli Ropeway, and scenic trekking trails make it a must-visit destination for nature enthusiasts.

MAJULI

Majuli, the world’s largest river island, located in Assam, is a haven of scenic beauty and vibrant culture. Surrounded by the mighty Brahmaputra River, it is famed for its Satras (monasteries), lush greenery, unique traditions, and captivating sunsets, making it a paradise for nature and culture lovers.

TAWANG

Tawang, nestled in the Arunachal Pradesh Himalayas, is renowned for its breathtaking landscapes, serene monasteries, and rich Buddhist heritage. Home to the iconic Tawang Monastery, it offers stunning views of snow-clad peaks, pristine lakes, and vibrant festivals, making it a perfect blend of peace and adventure.

VARANASI

Varanasi, the spiritual heart of India, is one of the world’s oldest cities, located along the sacred Ganges River. Renowned for its vibrant Ghats, mesmerizing Ganga Aarti, and rich cultural heritage, it’s a profound destination for seekers of spirituality, history, and timeless traditions.

CHAMPANER

Champaner, a UNESCO World Heritage Site in Gujarat, is a treasure trove of history and architecture. Nestled at the foothills of Pavagadh, it boasts stunning Islamic and Hindu monuments, intricate carvings, and ancient temples, offering a glimpse into India’s rich medieval past amidst serene landscapes.

HALABEIDU

Halebidu, located in Karnataka, is a jewel of Hoysala architecture. Famous for its stunning temples adorned with intricate carvings and sculptures, it offers a serene ambiance amidst lush greenery. Halebidu’s Chennakesava Temple is a testament to the artistry and rich history of the region.

BETALBATIM

Betalbatim, a serene beach in Goa, is known for its pristine golden sands and calm waters. Less crowded than other beaches, it offers a peaceful escape, perfect for sunbathing, swimming, and enjoying mesmerizing sunsets, making it an ideal destination for relaxation and tranquility.

HAMPI

Hampi, a UNESCO World Heritage Site in Karnataka, is a captivating blend of ancient ruins and natural beauty. Famous for its historical temples, stone monuments, and vibrant marketplaces, it offers a glimpse into the grandeur of the Vijayanagara Empire amidst stunning landscapes of boulders and hills.

SUNDARBANS

The Sundarbans, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is a vast mangrove forest straddling India and Bangladesh. Famous for its unique wildlife, including the Bengal tiger, it offers a thrilling adventure in its dense jungles, winding waterways, and rich biodiversity, making it a nature lover’s paradise.
